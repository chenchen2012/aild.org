## What changed this week

- AI governance is recognized as a strategic priority, with successful AI initiatives beginning with control frameworks rather than just code development.
- Demand for chief AI officers is accelerating, indicating a shift toward dedicated AI leadership roles in the C-suite.
- Senior management often loses confidence in AI before it reaches scale, highlighting governance and trust gaps.

## Leadership implications

- Leaders must prioritize AI governance and risk mitigation, using frameworks like COSO to establish control and build trust.
- Human-led, AI-powered leadership is emerging as a model, requiring alignment between human decision-makers and AI systems.
- Cross-functional leadership alignment, especially insights from women across the enterprise, is critical for AI success.

## Decisions for the next 7 days

- Assess current AI governance structures and consider appointing or empowering a chief AI officer to oversee strategy.
- Review AI risk mitigation plans using step-by-step guides like COSO to address emerging technology risks.
- Initiate discussions on leadership alignment for AI initiatives, ensuring diverse perspectives are included in decision-making.
