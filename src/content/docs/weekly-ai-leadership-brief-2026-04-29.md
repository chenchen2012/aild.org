## What changed this week

- Connected data is now seen as essential for AI decision-ready sustainability, creating a 'trust dividend' for organizations that invest in data integration.
- Experts emphasize that governing AI at scale requires unified data control to build trust, moving beyond siloed approaches.
- New podcasts and thought leadership highlight the shift toward AI, quantum, and new rules of decision-making, urging executives to rethink operating models.

## Leadership implications

- Executives must champion unified data governance as a strategic enabler, not just a compliance requirement, to unlock AI's full potential.
- Delaying AI architecture decisions—especially around data integration and control—creates costly rework and erodes trust.
- Closing the gap between AI and enterprise systems requires deliberate actions: aligning AI initiatives with business processes, investing in data quality, and fostering cross-functional collaboration.

## Decisions for the next 7 days

- Assess your organization's current data governance maturity and identify silos that hinder AI scalability.
- Initiate a cross-functional task force to define a unified data control framework for AI, involving IT, data science, and business leaders.
- Review pending AI architecture decisions and prioritize investments in data integration and interoperability to avoid future bottlenecks.
