## What changed this week

- SAS was named a leader in AI Governance by Chartis, highlighting the growing importance of formal governance frameworks.
- A new partnership between Yubico, Auth0, and IBM aims to secure agentic applications, addressing security risks in AI systems.
- Bedrock Data expanded ArgusAI to govern enterprise AI risk surfaces, indicating increased focus on risk management tools.

## Leadership implications

- AI governance is becoming a competitive differentiator, requiring investment in frameworks and tools to ensure trusted innovation.
- Measuring AI ROI remains challenging; enterprises need clear benchmarks beyond cost savings to evaluate success effectively.
- Scaling AI agents requires treating them like team members with defined roles and integration into existing workflows.

## Decisions for the next 7 days

- Review current AI governance frameworks against emerging standards and tools like those from SAS and Bedrock Data.
- Establish clear metrics for AI ROI that include speed, scale, and control improvements, especially in finance functions.
- Assess security protocols for AI agents and consider partnerships or tools to address risks in agentic applications.
