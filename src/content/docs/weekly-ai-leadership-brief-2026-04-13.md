## What changed this week

- Managers and executives show significant disagreement on AI priorities and implementation, creating operational costs and misalignment (Source 1).
- Weak AI governance is identified as the biggest risk in enterprise automation, highlighting urgent need for robust frameworks (Source 3).
- AI is increasingly viewed not as software but as enterprise infrastructure, requiring new operational models and investment approaches (Source 9).

## Leadership implications

- CIOs must integrate governance into enterprise AI strategies to ensure compliance, risk management, and ethical deployment (Source 2).
- Data quality assessment frameworks (like 3-step approaches for unstructured data) become critical for AI reliability and decision-making accuracy (Source 8).
- Cross-functional collaboration (exemplified by Microsoft's data council) is essential for unified data strategies and effective AI implementation (Source 4).

## Decisions for the next 7 days

- Assess alignment between managerial and executive AI priorities through structured discussions to reduce costly disagreements (Source 1).
- Review current AI governance frameworks against emerging risks in automation and plan immediate strengthening measures (Source 3).
- Initiate evaluation of AI as infrastructure component, considering partnerships (like OpenText on AWS Sovereign Cloud) for scalable, compliant solutions (Source 5).
