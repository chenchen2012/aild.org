## What changed this week

- Research reveals significant disagreement between managers and executives on AI priorities, costing companies in implementation efficiency.
- Weak AI governance is identified as the top risk in enterprise automation, surpassing technical failures.
- Microsoft demonstrates how a data council can unify AI strategy, highlighting structured governance approaches.
- New frameworks emerge for SME AI adoption, emphasizing structured implementation over ad-hoc solutions.

## Leadership implications

- Misalignment between leadership levels creates implementation bottlenecks; executives must bridge this gap through clear communication.
- Governance deficiencies expose organizations to regulatory and operational risks; CIOs must integrate governance into AI initiatives from the start.
- Cloud strategy decisions are increasingly driven by governance capabilities rather than just cost or performance metrics.
- The 'human exception' in AI governance remains inadequately addressed, risking compliance failures and ethical breaches.

## Decisions for the next 7 days

- Conduct an alignment workshop between executives and managers to identify and resolve AI priority disagreements.
- Review current AI governance frameworks against emerging best practices, focusing on human oversight mechanisms.
- Assess cloud strategy alignment with AI governance requirements, particularly for data-sensitive operations.
- Initiate a pilot structured AI framework for one business unit to test governance integration before broader rollout.
