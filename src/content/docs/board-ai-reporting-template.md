## What should a board AI reporting template include?

A board AI reporting template should show business value, governance posture, unresolved risks, incidents, and next management decisions in a format directors can review quickly without losing oversight quality.

## Why this matters for executive teams

Boards often receive AI updates that either feel too operational or too abstract. A template keeps reporting focused on the oversight questions directors actually need answered.

## Core reporting sections

### 1. Executive summary

- what changed since the last board review
- where AI is creating measurable value
- which issues need director attention this cycle

### 2. Value and performance

- outcome metrics tied to named initiatives
- quality or productivity evidence, not just usage volume
- initiatives recommended to scale, pause, or redesign

### 3. Risk and control posture

- current governance status
- control gaps or exceptions
- incidents, near misses, and remediation progress

### 4. Decisions and escalation

- issues requiring immediate board visibility
- management decisions pending this quarter
- questions the board should press management on next

## Common reporting mistakes

- reporting activity without oversight implications
- separating value metrics from governance context
- burying incidents or exceptions in appendix material
- ending the update without a clear next decision
