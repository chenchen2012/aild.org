## What changed this week

- 72% of enterprises lack the AI governance and security controls they believe they have, revealing a significant oversight gap (Source 2).
- INSEAD and KPMG launched global AI Board Governance Principles, emphasizing board oversight as AI reshapes corporate leadership (Source 8).
- Alteryx introduced an AI Insights Agent on Google Cloud Marketplace, aiming to provide reliable, repeatable AI answers for enterprises (Source 10).

## Leadership implications

- AI success depends more on execution and operating models than on tools alone; leaders must prioritize implementation over technology selection (Source 1).
- Governance and innovation are not opposing forces; effective AI strategies integrate both to ensure control without stifling progress (Source 4).
- Measuring AI ROI should focus on hidden metrics like process efficiency and decision confidence, not just direct financial returns (Source 12).

## Decisions for the next 7 days

- Audit current AI governance frameworks against the new INSEAD-KPMG principles to identify and address control gaps (Source 2, 8).
- Review AI project portfolios to shift focus from tool acquisition to execution excellence, ensuring alignment with business outcomes (Source 1, 3).
- Initiate discussions on AI agent deployment strategies, learning from frontier firms like Microsoft to enhance operational models (Source 9).
