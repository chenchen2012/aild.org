## What changed this week

- 72% of enterprises lack effective AI governance despite believing they have control (source 2).
- INSEAD and KPMG launched global AI Board Governance Principles to guide oversight (source 11).
- Experts argue AI governance and innovation must be integrated, not opposing forces (source 3).

## Leadership implications

- Boards must move beyond passive oversight to active governance frameworks that balance risk and innovation (source 11).
- Delaying architecture decisions (e.g., agentic vs. pipeline AI) creates technical debt that slows velocity (source 9, 10).
- Unified data control is foundational for trust and scaling AI governance (source 4).

## Decisions for the next 7 days

- Audit current AI governance maturity against the 72% gap; identify specific control weaknesses (source 2).
- Evaluate architecture choices for AI workflows—prioritize scalable, workflow-first designs (source 8).
- Assign an executive owner for AI governance to ensure alignment with innovation goals (source 3).
