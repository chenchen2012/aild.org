## What changed this week

- AI-native planning is now critical for supply chain leaders, requiring immediate operating model shifts to integrate AI into core processes.
- Enterprise AI economics show measurable ROI through platforms like Microsoft Foundry, with Forrester TEI studies revealing significant efficiency gains.
- CISO roles are evolving due to AI, with reports indicating increased focus on AI security governance and risk management frameworks.

## Leadership implications

- Leaders must adopt 'thinking mode' with AI to enhance strategic decision-making, moving beyond automation to collaborative intelligence.
- Human-led, AI-powered leadership models are emerging, emphasizing the need for leaders to guide AI integration while maintaining human oversight.
- Trust in AI agents requires robust governance frameworks, as highlighted by experts advancing enterprise resilience through AI governance standards.

## Decisions for the next 7 days

- Review and optimize IT infrastructure for AI readiness, ensuring scalability and security to support AI-native operations.
- Establish or refine AI governance committees to address decision-making, risk, and ethical guidelines, aligning with 2026 human capital trends.
- Initiate pilot projects for AI-powered financial decision-making, leveraging AI to reimagine work and value creation in key business units.
