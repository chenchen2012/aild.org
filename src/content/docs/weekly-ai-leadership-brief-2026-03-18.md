## What changed this week

- AI governance is recognized as a strategic priority, with successful AI initiatives beginning with control frameworks rather than just code development.
- Chartis named SAS a leader in AI Governance, highlighting the growing importance of structured governance solutions.
- Senior management often loses confidence in AI before it reaches scale due to governance and operational challenges.

## Leadership implications

- Leaders must prioritize AI governance to build trust and ensure ethical, compliant deployments.
- Alignment across leadership teams is critical for AI success, as highlighted by insights from women across enterprises.
- Scaling enterprise AI requires maturity in data science and machine learning capabilities, not just technological investment.

## Decisions for the next 7 days

- Review and strengthen AI governance frameworks to align with strategic goals and regulatory expectations.
- Assess data science and machine learning maturity to identify gaps hindering AI scalability.
- Foster leadership alignment through cross-functional discussions to ensure cohesive AI strategy and execution.
