## What changed this week

- 72% of enterprises lack the AI control and security they believe they have, revealing a governance mirage (source 3).
- INSEAD and KPMG launched global AI Board Governance Principles, emphasizing board oversight of AI strategy and risk (source 12).
- BMO launched 'Return on Intelligence' podcast, focusing on AI, quantum, and new rules of decision-making (source 6).
- Enterprise AI leaders prioritize execution over tools, focusing on workflow-first architecture and scalable value (sources 2, 9).
- CIOs who delay AI architecture decisions face higher costs and integration challenges later (source 8).

## Leadership implications

- Boards must move beyond passive oversight to active governance, integrating AI risk into fiduciary duties (sources 3, 12).
- AI governance and innovation are not opposing forces; unified data control is essential for trust and scale (sources 4, 5).
- Execution-focused operating models outperform tool-centric approaches; leaders invest in workflow integration and data architecture (sources 2, 9).
- Decision-making frameworks must evolve to incorporate AI-driven insights, as highlighted by BMO's new podcast (source 6).
- Data governance must be reimagined for AI era, moving from static policies to dynamic, context-aware controls (source 11).

## Decisions for the next 7 days

- Assess your organization's AI governance maturity: do you have unified data control and board-level oversight? (sources 3, 5, 12).
- Review your AI architecture roadmap; avoid delaying decisions on data integration and workflow orchestration (sources 8, 9).
- Shift focus from AI tools to execution: identify one workflow where AI can deliver measurable value this quarter (sources 2, 9).
- Engage with the new INSEAD/KPMG principles to benchmark your board's AI governance practices (source 12).
- Listen to BMO's 'Return on Intelligence' podcast to explore new decision-making paradigms (source 6).
