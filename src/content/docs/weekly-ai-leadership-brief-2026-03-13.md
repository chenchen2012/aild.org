## What changed this week

- AI governance is increasingly recognized as a strategic priority, with successful initiatives beginning with control frameworks rather than just technical implementation.
- Enterprise AI leadership now requires balancing curiosity with responsibility, focusing on ethical deployment and risk management.
- Organizations are establishing dedicated governance bodies, such as global CISO councils, to address AI risks and emerging technology threats.

## Leadership implications

- Leaders must shift from viewing AI as purely technical to seeing governance as integral to strategy and value realization.
- CISOs and security leaders are taking on expanded roles in AI governance, requiring collaboration across functions.
- Senior management confidence in AI depends on clear governance, measurable outcomes, and transparent risk mitigation.

## Decisions for the next 7 days

- Review and align AI initiatives with established governance frameworks like COSO to mitigate risks systematically.
- Initiate cross-functional discussions to define AI responsibility and accountability within your operating model.
- Assess current AI governance maturity and identify gaps in control mechanisms or leadership oversight.
